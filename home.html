About Me!

My name is Guled Ahmed. I was born in Somalia in 1988. In 1991, a civil war broke out in Somalia and my family had to fled the country. We seeked refuge and safety in a neighboring country called Ethiopia.

In Ethiopia, we moved several times from one city to another, and we finally settled in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia's capital city. We began to participate in a United States refugee process where refugees were being airlifted and resettled in the U.S. My aunt sponsored us. She was living somewhere in the U.S at the time.

In 1996, we were settled in San Diego, California. This is where most of my memories began. I begin to attend elementary school in San Diego. I grew up in San Diego's City Heights community, formerly known as East San Diego. There are over 80 different langauges that are spoken in City Heights. It is one of the most diverse community in the country.

I was never into coding prior to being exposed to coding in my pre-workout assignments for the UCSD Trilogy bootcamp. I found that coding was very addictive. The amount of work that goes in to coding is a lot, but time flies by so fast because it's so fun. After doing my pre-workout, I was so eager to start the coding program. I wanted to learn more. I want to expand my knowledge. I knew that the more I knew how to code and how coding works, the more fun I would be having.

I like making new friends and getting to know people. You can reach me at my Github page or you can email me at a.kindness.to.remember@gmail.com
